Sorbonne ENGL 102 Writing in the Humanities

Course Description

This course helps military cadets improve their writing skills while learning about leadership through interesting stories, movies, and real examples. Students will read short articles, watch films, and write essays about leadership within the humanities, in history, literature, and modern life. The focus is on clear communication and understanding what makes a good leader.

Course Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

  1. Read and understand leadership stories and articles
  2. Write clear essays about leadership topics
  3. Discuss leadership ideas in class
  4. Analyze what makes leaders successful or unsuccessful

Assignments

Major Assessments (70% of final grade)

Essay 1: My Favorite Leader (300 words)

Write about a leader you admire and explain why. Use examples from class readings or films.

Due: Week 5

Essay 2: Learning from Mistakes (400 words)

Analyze a leader who made a serious mistake. What did they do wrong and what can we learn?

Due: Week 9

Essay 3: Leadership Today (500 words)

Compare leadership in the past to leadership today. Use examples from course materials.

Due: Week 12

Final Reflection (200 words)

Write about what you learned about leadership and yourself in this course.

Due: Week 14

Weekly Activities (30% of final grade)

  • Short reading responses (100 words each)
  • Film discussions
  • Group activities
  • Class participation

Schedule

Unit 1: Introduction to Leadership (Weeks 1-3)

Week 1: What Makes a Good Leader?

Focus: Understanding leadership basics

  • Readings:
    • Simple leadership article from UAE
    • “Great Leaders” short article
  • Film: Short clips about famous leaders
  • Writing: “A Leader I Know” (personal story)
  • Discussion: Leaders you know

Week 2: Historical Leaders

Focus: Learning from the past

  • Readings:
    • Story about Sheikh Zayed
    • Brief story about George Washington
  • Film: “The King’s Speech” (selected parts)
  • Writing: Compare two leaders
  • Discussion: How do leaders inspire people?

Week 3: Modern Leadership

Focus: Leadership in today’s world

  • Readings:
    • Article about UAE leaders today
    • Story about a modern leader
  • Film: Clips about modern business leaders
  • Writing: Essay 1 draft workshop
  • Discussion: Can anyone be a leader?

Unit 2: Learning from Success (Weeks 4-6)

Week 4: Inspiring Others

Focus: How leaders motivate people

  • Readings:
    • Story about Nelson Mandela
    • Article about motivation
  • Film: “Invictus” (parts about reconciliation)
  • Writing: Essay 1 revision
  • Discussion: What motivates you to follow a leader?

Week 5: Making Tough Decisions

Focus: Leadership in difficult times

  • Readings:
    • Article about wartime decisions
    • Story about crisis leadership
  • Film: “Saving Private Ryan” (leadership scenes)
  • Writing: Submit Essay 1: My Favorite Leader
  • Discussion: When is it okay to make unpopular decisions?

Week 6: UAE Leadership

Focus: Leadership in the UAE context

  • Readings:
    • Sheikh Zayed’s philosophy
    • Article about UAE Vision 2071
  • Film: Documentary about UAE development
  • Writing: Personal reflection on UAE leadership
  • Discussion: How does UAE leadership reflect its culture?

Unit 3: Learning from Failure (Weeks 7-9)

Week 7: When Leaders Fail

Focus: Understanding leadership mistakes

  • Readings:
    • Story about a leader’s failure
    • Article about learning from mistakes
  • Film: “The Social Network” (leadership challenges)
  • Writing: Essay 2 planning
  • Discussion: Why do good leaders sometimes fail?

Week 8: Ethics and Leadership

Focus: Right and wrong in leadership

  • Readings:
    • Simple article about ethical leadership
    • Story about a moral choice
  • Film: “Letters from Iwo Jima” (honor across cultures)
  • Writing: Essay 2 workshop
  • Discussion: What would you do in this situation?

Week 9: Recovery and Redemption

Focus: Can leaders recover from failure?

  • Readings:
    • Story about leader’s comeback
    • Article about second chances
  • Film: Clips about resilience
  • Writing: Submit Essay 2: Learning from Mistakes
  • Discussion: Is redemption possible for leaders?

Unit 4: Leadership Today (Weeks 10-12)

Week 10: Team Leadership

Focus: Leading groups and teams

  • Readings:
    • Article about teamwork
    • Story about military teamwork
  • Film: “Apollo 13” (team leadership)
  • Writing: Essay 3 planning
  • Discussion: What makes a good team leader?

Week 11: Technology and Leadership

Focus: Leadership in the digital age

  • Readings:
    • Article about tech leaders
    • Story about digital transformation
  • Film: Clips about modern innovation
  • Writing: Essay 3 draft workshop
  • Discussion: How has technology changed leadership?

Week 12: Global Leadership

Focus: Leadership across cultures

  • Readings:
    • Article about international leaders
    • Story about cross-cultural leadership
  • Film: Documentary about global leaders
  • Writing: Submit Essay 3: Leadership Today
  • Discussion: What makes a leader work across cultures?

Unit 5: Your Leadership Future (Weeks 13-14)

Week 13: Leadership in the UAE

Focus: Your role as a future leader

  • Readings:
    • Article about UAE’s future leaders
    • Vision for young Emiratis
  • Film: “The Message” (Islamic leadership principles)
  • Writing: Final reflection planning
  • Discussion: What kind of leader do you want to be?

Week 14: Course Reflection

Focus: What did you learn?

  • Activities:
    • Share favorite insights from course
    • Discuss how course changed your thinking
    • Peer feedback on essays
  • Writing: Final Reflection Submission
  • Discussion: Your leadership goals for the future

This course is designed specifically for first-year cadets at Zayed Military University. It focuses on building basic writing skills while learning about leadership through accessible stories and examples relevant to UAE culture and military life.

Grading System

Essay Grading (Simple 4-Point Scale)

  1. Excellent - Clear ideas, good examples, well-organized
  2. Good - Clear main points, some examples, mostly organized
  3. Needs Improvement - Some good ideas but unclear or disorganized
  4. Poor - Unclear ideas, missing examples, poorly organized

Class Participation

  • Coming prepared with readings completed
  • Contributing to class discussions
  • Working well in group activities
  • Being respectful to classmates and instructor
