Sorbonne ENGL 102 Writing in the Humanities

Course Description

This course helps military cadets improve their writing skills while learning about leadership through interesting stories, movies, and real-world examples. Students will read short articles, watch clips from films, and write essays about leadership within the humanities, in history, literature, and modern life. The focus is on clear communication and understanding what makes a good leader.

Learning Outcomes

  1. Read and understand leadership stories and articles.
  2. Write clear essays about leadership topics.
  3. Discuss leadership ideas in class.
  4. Analyze what makes leaders successful or unsuccessful.

Major Assessments

  • Essay 1: My Favorite Leader (500 words)
  • Essay 2: Learning from Mistakes (800 words)
  • Essay 3: Leadership Today (1000 words)
  • Final Reflection (200 words)
  • Weekly Activities and Class Participation

Assignments

Writing 1: An Interesting Leader** (500 words - 20% of grade)

Purpose: Develop personal connection to leadership concepts through admiration and analysis.
Task: Write about a leader you admire and explain why. Use examples from class readings or films.

Requirements:

  • Clear description of the leader and their qualities
  • Specific examples from course materials
  • Personal reflection on why this leader inspires you
  • Well-organized structure with clear introduction and conclusion

Writing 2: Learning from Mistakes (800 words - 30% of grade)

Purpose: Analyze leadership failures to understand what not to do and recovery possibilities.
Task: Analyze a leader who made a serious mistake. What did they do wrong and what can we learn?

Requirements:

  • Detailed analysis of the leader’s mistake
  • Critical evaluation of the consequences
  • Lessons learned and applications to modern leadership
  • Proper use of course readings and film examples

Writing 3: Leadership Today (1000 words - 30% of grade)

Purpose: Compare historical and contemporary leadership to understand evolution and adaptation.
Task: Compare leadership in the past to leadership today. Use examples from course materials.

Requirements:

  • Clear comparison between past and present leadership
  • Use of multiple course examples and sources
  • Analysis of what has changed and what remains constant
  • Conclusion about effective leadership across time periods

Presentation (5–10 minutes - 10% of grade)

Purpose: Present an original idea from research related to the theme of the class.

Requirements:

  • Explain research question, thesis, and main points
  • Discuss supporting evidence
  • Address one counterargument
  • Use slides or visual aids if needed

Final Portfolio (10% of grade)

Purpose: Synthesize learning and personal growth throughout the course.
Task: Write about what you learned about leadership and yourself in this course.

Requirements:

  • Personal insights about leadership development
  • Write a 200-word description of leadership, as addressed in this class
  • Connection to your own leadership goals
  • Reflection on course impact and changed perspectives
  • Clear and concise writing

Schedule

Week 1: Orientation & Leadership Basics

  • Course overview and expectations
  • Introduction to leadership concepts
  • “A Leader I Know” personal writing exercise
  • Discussion: Leaders you know and admire

Week 2: Historical Leadership Inspiration

  • Reading: How Eisenhower’s Crisis Management Strategies Will Help You Master the Art of Leadership
  • Film: “The King’s Speech” (selected parts)
  • Writing: Compare two leaders exercise
  • Discussion: How do leaders inspire people?

Week 3: Modern Leadership Context

  • Reading: Government Leaders, What Makes a Leader?
  • Film: Clips about modern business leaders
  • Writing: Essay 1 draft workshop
  • Discussion: Can anyone be a leader?

Week 4: Inspiring Others

  • Reading: 3 Ways to Motivate Your Team Through an Extended Crisis
  • Film: “Invictus” (reconciliation leadership parts)
  • Writing: Essay 1 revision workshop
  • Discussion: What motivates you to follow a leader?

Week 5: Leadership in Difficult Times

  • Reading: Leadership Lessons From the Wartime General, Leadership in Crisis: 10 Key Strategies
  • Film: “Saving Private Ryan” (leadership scenes)
  • Writing: Submit Essay 1: My Favorite Leader
  • Discussion: When is it okay to make unpopular decisions?

Week 6: UAE Leadership Context

  • Reading: UAE Centennial 2071
  • Film: Documentary about UAE development
  • Writing: Personal reflection on UAE leadership
  • Discussion: How does UAE leadership reflect its culture?

Week 7: Understanding Leadership Mistakes

  • Reading: Why Good Leaders Fail, Strategies for Learning from Failure
  • Film: “The Social Network” (leadership challenges)
  • Writing: Essay 2 planning workshop
  • Discussion: Why do good leaders sometimes fail?

Week 8: Ethics in Leadership

  • Reading: A New Model for Ethical Leadership, A Framework for Ethical Decision Making
  • Film: “Letters from Iwo Jima” (honor across cultures)
  • Writing: Essay 2 workshop and peer review
  • Discussion: What would you do in this situation?

Week 9: Recovery and Redemption

  • Reading: How to Turn Setbacks into Comebacks, Redemption in Leadership: Lessons for Workplace Growth
  • Film: Clips about resilience and recovery
  • Writing: Submit Essay 2: Learning from Mistakes
  • Discussion: Is redemption possible for leaders?

Week 10: Team Leadership

  • Reading: Google re:Work - Understand Team Effectiveness, Army Leaders Discuss Readiness, Teamwork
  • Film: “Apollo 13” (team leadership)
  • Writing: Essay 3 planning workshop
  • Discussion: What makes a good team leader?

Week 11: Leadership in the Digital Age

  • Reading: Leadership Development “Hacks” from Silicon Valley, The New Elements of Digital Transformation
  • Film: Clips about modern innovation
  • Writing: Essay 3 draft workshop
  • Discussion: How has technology changed leadership?

Week 12: Global Leadership

  • Reading: Cross-cultural Leadership
  • Film: Documentary about global leaders
  • Writing: Submit Essay 3: Leadership Today
  • Discussion: What makes a leader work across cultures?

Week 13: Leadership in the UAE Context

  • Reading: Federal Youth Authority
  • Film: “The Message” (Islamic leadership principles)
  • Writing: Final reflection planning
  • Discussion: What kind of leader do you want to be?

Week 14: Course Reflection and Future Goals

  • Course summary and learning synthesis
  • Sharing favorite insights from the course
  • Peer feedback on essays and final reflection
  • Submit Final Reflection
  • Discussion: Your leadership goals for the future

Course Policies

Attendance: Required - Military training values discipline. More than 3 absences may affect your grade. Contact instructor if you miss class due to military duties.
Academic Integrity: All submitted work must be original and properly cited.
Late Work: May be penalized unless approved in advance.
Accessibility: Students requiring accommodations should contact Student Affairs.

  • Simple Leadership Stories - Collection of easy-to-read articles
  • UAE Leadership Examples - Stories about UAE leaders
  • Historical Leadership Tales - Brief stories about famous leaders
  • Modern Leadership Articles - Current leadership examples
  • “The King’s Speech” (selected scenes)
  • “Saving Private Ryan” (leadership scenes)
  • “Invictus” (reconciliation leadership)
  • “Apollo 13” (team leadership)
