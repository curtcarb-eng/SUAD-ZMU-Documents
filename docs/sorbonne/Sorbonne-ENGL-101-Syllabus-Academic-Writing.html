Sorbonne ENGL 101 Syllabus Academic Writing

Course Description

This course develops academic writing, reading, and critical thinking skills. Students practice the full writing process, produce multiple writings across rhetorical modes, learn research methods, and build the foundation for university-level communication.

Learning Outcomes

  1. Produce clear, coherent academic prose.
  2. Construct thesis-driven writings in narrative, analytical, and argumentative forms.
  3. Read critically, summarize, paraphrase, and respond to texts.
  4. Locate, evaluate, and integrate academic sources responsibly.
  5. Apply the writing process: planning, drafting, revising, editing.
  6. Produce a research-supported argumentative writing.
  7. Present research findings in an organized oral format.

  • Week 8: Drafting Writing 2; integrating evidence.

Assignments

Writing 1: Exploratory Writing (500 words - 20% of grade)

Purpose: Develop clear structure and reflective exploratory writing. Task: Write an exploratory writing related to the theme of the class that shaped your perspective.

Requirements:

  • Clear controlling idea
  • Narrative detail and reflection
  • Logical structure
  • Edited for clarity and grammar

Writing 2: Textual/Rhetorical Analysis (800 words - 30% of grade)

Purpose: Strengthen critical reading and analytical writing. Task: Analyze how an author persuades an audience. Focus on rhetorical strategies.

Requirements:

  • Thesis with analytical claim
  • Use of rhetorical frameworks
  • Evidence from text (quotes/paraphrase)
  • Focus on how the text persuades

Writing 3: Research-Supported Argument (1000 words - 30% of grade)

Purpose: Produce a sustained argument using credible sources. Task: Answer a focused research question using 3–5 academic or high-quality sources.

Requirements:

  • Clear argument and counterargument
  • Integrate sources with citations
  • Logical organization
  • Academic tone

Research Presentation (5–10 minutes - 10% of grade)

Purpose: Present research findings clearly and persuasively.

Requirements:

  • Explain research question, thesis, and main points
  • Discuss supporting evidence
  • Address one counterargument
  • Use slides or visual aids if needed

Final Portfolio (- 10% of grade)

Purpose: Demonstrate growth across the course through revision and reflection. Contents:

  • Revised writings (as required)
  • Reflective writing

Week 1: Orientation & Diagnostic

  • Course overview and expectations
  • Academic integrity introduction
  • Diagnostic writing task

Week 2: Academic Reading & Paragraph Skills

  • Active reading strategies
  • Summary writing
  • Paragraph structure and cohesion
  • Short summary assignment

Week 3: Writing Fundamentals

  • Thesis development
  • Writing structure
  • Writing 1 outline submission

Week 4: Drafting Writing 1

  • Drafting workshop
  • Peer review
  • Revision strategies

Week 5: Introduction to Rhetorical Analysis

  • Purpose, audience, tone
  • Ethos, pathos, logos
  • In-class rhetorical analysis task

Week 6: Critical Reading & Analytical Writing

  • Identifying claims, assumptions, evidence
  • Analytical response assignment

Week 7: Planning Writing 2

  • Close reading techniques
  • Rhetorical frameworks (SOAP, rhetorical triangle)
  • Writing 2 proposal

Week 8: Drafting Writing 2

  • Integrating quotations and commentary
  • Peer review and revision

Week 9: Research Foundations

  • Research questions and keywords
  • Library/database strategies
  • Research scavenger hunt

Week 10: Evaluating Sources & Citation Skills

  • Source credibility evaluation
  • Paraphrasing and avoiding plagiarism
  • Annotated bibliography submission

Week 11: Argument Structure

  • Claims, reasons, evidence
  • Counterargument strategies
  • Writing 3 argument map

Week 12: Drafting Writing 3

  • Synthesizing sources
  • Long-form structure
  • Peer review

Week 13: Revision & Portfolio Preparation

  • Writing 3 final submission
  • Portfolio revision planning

Week 14: Research Presentations

  • Presentation guidelines
  • In-class presentations

Week 15: Portfolio Workshop

  • Revision support
  • Reflective writing

Week 16: Final Portfolio Submission

  • Submission of all revised work
  • Reflective writing

Course Policies

Attendance: Active engagement is expected in all sessions. Academic Integrity: All submitted work must be original and properly cited. Late Work: May be penalized unless approved in advance. Accessibility: Students requiring accommodations should contact Student Affairs.
