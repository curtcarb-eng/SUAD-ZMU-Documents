ZMU ENGL101 Humanities Leadership Syllabus

Writing-Based First-Year Course for Military Cadets

Zayed Military University - Sorbonne University Abu Dhabi Partnership

Course Description

This writing-intensive humanities course explores leadership through diverse cultural, historical, and philosophical lenses specifically designed for military cadets. Students will analyze short texts, films, case studies, and primary sources to develop critical thinking skills essential for ethical leadership in military contexts. The course emphasizes analytical writing, persuasive communication, and moral reasoning while examining leadership challenges across different eras, cultures, and situations.

Course Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

  1. Analyze Leadership Approaches: Critically evaluate different leadership philosophies and their applications in military contexts
  2. Write Persuasive Arguments: Compose clear, evidence-based arguments about leadership decisions and their consequences
  3. Synthesize Diverse Perspectives: Integrate insights from literature, history, philosophy, and contemporary sources
  4. Apply Ethical Frameworks: Use ethical reasoning to analyze complex leadership dilemmas
  5. Communicate Effectively: Present complex ideas clearly in both written and oral formats
  6. Connect Theory to Practice: Apply humanities insights to contemporary military leadership challenges

Major Assessments (60% of final grade)

Essay 1: Historical Leadership Analysis (800 words)

Analyze a historical military leader’s decision-making process using primary sources and historical accounts. Focus on ethical considerations, cultural context, and long-term consequences.

Due: Week 5

Essay 2: Literature and Leadership Character Study (1000 words)

Analyze how a literary work or short story explores leadership themes such as courage, duty, moral ambiguity, or sacrifice. Connect insights to modern military leadership challenges.

Due: Week 9

Essay 3: Contemporary Leadership Case Study (1200 words)

Examine a contemporary leadership situation (military, business, or civic) using multiple perspectives from course materials. Include ethical analysis and propose alternative approaches.

Due: Week 12

Final Portfolio and Reflection (500 words)

Submit revised versions of all three essays with a reflective essay on your development as a critical thinker and future leader.

Due: Week 14

Weekly Writing Tasks (40% of final grade)

  • Weekly reading responses (200-300 words)
  • Film analysis worksheets
  • Peer review exercises
  • Short argumentative paragraphs
  • Discussion leadership roles

14-Week Course Schedule

Unit 1: Foundations of Leadership (Weeks 1-3)

Week 1: What is Leadership?

Focus: Introduction to leadership studies through humanities lens

  • Readings:
    • “Why the Military Produces Great Leaders” (Leadership articles)
    • “8 Essential Qualities of Successful Leaders” (Leadership articles)
  • Film: Selected clips from “12 Strong” or “American Sniper”
  • Writing: Diagnostic essay on personal leadership experience
  • Discussion: Leadership in UAE cultural context

Week 2: Ancient Wisdom on Leadership

Focus: Classical texts and military leadership principles

  • Readings:
    • Sun Tzu: “The Art of War” (selected excerpts)
    • Machiavelli: “The Prince” (leadership and power sections)
  • Film: “Lawrence of Arabia” (selected scenes)
  • Writing: Comparative analysis of Eastern vs. Western leadership philosophy
  • Discussion: How ancient wisdom applies to modern military leadership

Week 3: Ethics and Military Leadership

Focus: Moral decision-making in military contexts

  • Readings:
    • “Just War Theory” excerpts (Augustine, Aquinas)
    • “Leadership in a Combat Zone” (Leadership articles)
  • Case Study: Abu Ghraib scandal - ethical breakdown
  • Writing: Ethical analysis of military decision-making scenarios
  • Discussion: Cultural relativism vs. universal moral principles

Unit 2: Historical Perspectives (Weeks 4-6)

Week 4: Revolutionary Leaders

Focus: Leadership during times of radical change

  • Readings:
    • George Washington’s leadership letters
    • Ho Chi Minh speeches and writings
  • Film: “The King’s Speech” (leadership under pressure)
  • Writing: Essay 1 Workshop - Historical Leadership Analysis
  • Discussion: Leadership in independence movements vs. maintaining order

Week 5: World War Leaders

Focus: Leadership in global conflict

  • Readings:
    • Churchill’s wartime speeches
    • Eisenhower’s leadership principles
  • Film: “Saving Private Ryan” (leadership in crisis)
  • Writing: Submit Essay 1: Historical Leadership Analysis
  • Discussion: Democracy vs. authoritarian leadership in wartime

Week 6: Post-Colonial Leadership

Focus: Leadership in newly independent nations

  • Readings:
    • Nelson Mandela: “Long Walk to Freedom” excerpts
    • UAE leadership development documents
  • Film: “Invictus” (reconciliation leadership)
  • Writing: Character analysis of transformational leaders
  • Discussion: Leadership style differences between cultures

Unit 3: Literature and Character (Weeks 7-9)

Week 7: Military Literature and Honor

Focus: How literature explores military values

  • Readings:
    • “The Red Badge of Courage” excerpts (Crane)
    • “All Quiet on the Western Front” excerpts (Remarque)
  • Film: “Letters from Iwo Jima” (honor across cultures)
  • Writing: Character analysis of fictional military leaders
  • Discussion: Literature vs. history in depicting war

Week 8: Leadership and Moral Complexity

Focus: Ambiguous moral situations in leadership

  • Readings:
    • “The Things They Carried” excerpts (O’Brien)
    • “Catch-22” excerpts (Heller)
  • Film: “MAS*H” (moral complexity in military context)
  • Writing: Essay 2 Workshop - Literature and Leadership Character Study
  • Discussion: Can bad decisions still be leadership?

Week 9: Sacrifice and Service

Focus: Personal cost of leadership

  • Readings:
    • Audie Murphy’s autobiography excerpts
    • Medal of Honor citations and stories
  • Film: “We Were Soldiers” (leadership under fire)
  • Writing: Submit Essay 2: Literature and Leadership Character Study
  • Discussion: Individual heroism vs. collective responsibility

Unit 4: Contemporary Applications (Weeks 10-12)

Week 10: Emotional Intelligence in Leadership

Focus: Modern understanding of leadership psychology

  • Readings:
    • “Why Emotional Intelligence Is Crucial For Effective Leadership” (Leadership articles)
    • “What Makes a Leader” - Daniel Goleman (HBR article)
  • Film: “The Social Network” (tech leadership)
  • Writing: Emotional intelligence self-assessment essay
  • Discussion: STEM vs. humanities in leadership development

Week 11: Adaptive Leadership

Focus: Leading through change and uncertainty

  • Readings:
    • “5 Principles to Guide Adaptive Leadership” (Leadership articles)
    • “Why Servant Leadership Is More Important Than Ever” (Leadership articles)
  • Case Study: COVID-19 response leadership
  • Writing: Contemporary leadership analysis draft
  • Discussion: Crisis leadership in the digital age

Week 12: Team Dynamics and Followership

Focus: Leadership as relationship and responsibility

  • Readings:
    • “In Praise of Followers” (Leadership articles)
    • “What Companies Can Learn from Military Teams” (Leadership articles)
  • Film: “Apollo 13” (team leadership under pressure)
  • Writing: Submit Essay 3: Contemporary Leadership Case Study
  • Discussion: Leader development vs. follower development

Unit 5: Integration and Reflection (Weeks 13-14)

Week 13: UAE Leadership Context

Focus: Leadership in the Gulf region and UAE military tradition

  • Readings:
    • UAE Vision 2071 leadership principles
    • Sheikh Zayed leadership philosophy
  • Film: “The Message” (early Islamic leadership)
  • Writing: Final portfolio workshop
  • Discussion: Balancing tradition and modernization in leadership

Week 14: Synthesis and Future Leadership

Focus: Integrating humanities insights for future leadership

  • Activities:
    • Student presentations on leadership evolution
    • Portfolio peer review sessions
  • Writing: Final Portfolio Submission with reflection essay
  • Discussion: Your leadership philosophy statement

Assessment Rubric Standards

Essay Grading Criteria (All Essays)

  1. Thesis and Argument (25%): Clear, arguable thesis with logical development
  2. Evidence and Analysis (30%): Appropriate use of course materials with critical analysis
  3. Organization and Structure (20%): Logical flow with effective transitions
  4. Style and Clarity (15%): Academic tone with precise language
  5. Mechanics and Format (10%): Grammar, citation, and formatting accuracy

Participation and Engagement Standards

  • Discussion Leadership: Prepared to lead class discussion on assigned topics
  • Peer Review: Constructive feedback on classmates’ work
  • Attendance: Regular, engaged participation in all class activities
  • Collaboration: Effective teamwork in group activities presentations

Course Policies

Academic Integrity

All work must be original and properly attributed. Plagiarism will result in course failure. Paraphrasing without citation is also academic dishonesty. When in doubt, cite your sources.

Late Work Policy

Essays may be submitted up to 48 hours late with 10% penalty per day. After 48 hours, late essays will not be accepted except in documented emergency situations.

Attendance Requirements

Military discipline requires consistent attendance. More than two absences may result in grade reduction. Three or more absences require meeting with instructor to discuss continued enrollment.

Revision Policy

Major essays may be revised and resubmitted within one week of receiving feedback. Revisions must demonstrate substantial improvement and include a revision letter explaining changes made.

Accommodation Services

Students requiring academic accommodations should contact Student Services during the first week of classes. Military service-related accommodations will be considered on a case-by-case basis.
